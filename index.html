Simple = Minimal

I really enjoyed reading these myths. I have heard a few of them, but learned a lot more. Whenever I design anything, I like a simple and minimal design. I like using a few colors and only showing the user what they need to see. I really liked this quote from Albert Einstein. This is a very good quote to follow. You want to make something as easy as possible without simplyfying too much, the action is no longer easy or intuitive.

Everything should be made as simple as possible, but not simpler.

UX Myth

White Space Is Wasted Space

I have heard this myth a lot. Some people believe you should utilize every available pixle on a screen with color, or images, etc... This seems logical, you have a limited resource (screen space), and you want to maximize your product or brand. Sites that do this have statistically worse website than those who use white space. A quick example that comes to mind is ESPN. Their legacy website used so much space and was very cluttered. The new one uses whitespace and is much more engaging and easy to navigate. I have tried to make good use of white space in this site to bring focus on the content.
